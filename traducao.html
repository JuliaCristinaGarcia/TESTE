Greek mythology

It is a set of stories, legends and myths that belonged to the imagination of ancient Greek civilization, populated by gods, legendary heroes and fantastic creatures. Related to religion, Greek mythology presented, in addition to the worshiped gods, explanations for fundamental themes, such as the emergence of the Universe and life.

Roman mythology

Roman mythology is the set of beliefs, stories, myths and legends that were told by the Romans in antiquity. They were passed down orally from generation to generation. The legends that made up Roman mythology were based on accounts of the origin of Rome, the gods, men and the phenomena of nature.

Egyptian mythology

Egyptian mythology brings together several myths, legends and stories that were part of the religious imagination in Ancient Egypt until the arrival of Christianity. Remember that in Antiquity the Egyptian religion was based on polytheism, that is, the worship of various gods that usually took place in the temples dedicated to them.

Germanic mythology

Germanic mythology, also called Norse mythology, Viking mythology or Scandinavian mythology, is the set of pre-Christian legends of the Scandinavian peoples, especially during the Viking Age, whose knowledge came to our days mainly through the Icelandic Ages of the 13th century. Most of the written sources came from the Scandinavian peoples who settled in Iceland.

Celtic mythology

It encompasses the endless pantheon of Celtic deities, present in the religion of the Celtic peoples of Western Europe, the Alps and even Galatia (present-day Turkey, land of the Galatians). The expressions of worship and rituals, the cycles of Nature and the mediation of the Druids with men also enter into this mythological dimension that only began to be diluted with Roman rule.

African Mythology

African mythology is related to the Nigerian religion, which has around 400 gods. And each one of them has its characteristics and its meanings. Therefore, it is possible to see that in the middle of African mythology there is a lot of diversity, including among the gods.

Japanese Mythology

Japanese mythology has a history that goes back over 2000 years. This mythology includes a large number of deities such as Izanami, Izagani, Amaterasu and Inari. They are gods, goddesses and spirits, whose stories concern the creation of the world, the founding of the islands of Japan and the activities of deities, humans, animals and magical creatures.

Brazilian Mythology

It is synonymous with Brazilian popular culture, and represents the community's social identity through its cultural, collective or individual creations; it is also an essential part of Brazil's culture. Brazilian mythology emerged from the connection between the world and nature. Deities are represented by elements such as moon, sun and thunder. Today, it is still possible to find cultures that follow these beliefs. Mainly in the hinterlands, in the north and northeast of the country
